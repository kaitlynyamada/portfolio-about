My Values

  1. Be a lifelong learner.
    Retain the mindset of a student and stay informed about emerging visual trends and advancements in technology. Monitor the actions and strategies of influential figures in your field to remain current with the latest developments. Consistently peruse relevant literature to construct a well of valuable ideas and motivation.
  2. Be receptive to feedback and criticism.
    It's crucial to create prototypes and conduct user-testing in order to validate designs and assess them in realistic scenarios. Pay attention to user feedback and don't become attached to design elements that don't resonate with them.
  3. Do your research
    Prioritize comprehending the client's business model, followed by gaining insight into its user base. Identify user pain points and develop solutions that address them, while also aligning with the company's overall business objectives.
  4. Keep it simple

    Achieving simplicity in design is a challenging task, but the benefits are worth the effort. Strive for simplicity and intuitiveness in your design and explore creative and unconventional approaches to problem-solving. The most effective interface is one that seamlessly integrates with the user's experience and goes unnoticed.
